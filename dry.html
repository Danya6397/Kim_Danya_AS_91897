Advice for Dry Skin

Check out these resources

Products that claim to be ‘hydrating’ may not be clinically proven to have this effect. Make sure you check the labels and ingredients to understand whether a product will truly be beneficial or not. Dry skin is known to get even rougher in conditions with low humidity, especially winter. It may be beneficial to find a heavier night time cream during these times or sleep with a humidifier by your bed side.

Get to know your skin

Dry skin is where your skin generally lacks the moisture and lipids (fats) needed to keep your skin barrier supple. This is different to dehydrated skin which occurs periodically with insufficient water intake but can easily be fixed.

Dry skin generally appears to be:
Flaky or rough
Tight
More common fine lines

Ingredients to look out for

Hyaluronic acid- although called an ‘acid’ it is an ingredient that will aid in drawing moisture into your skin and retaining it. It is best used with or even in moisturiser.
Glycerin- Another ingredient that has the same moisturising benefits. It can be applied generously and helps maintain hydration to prevent dryness throughout the day or night.
Shea butter- Rich in fatty acids, shea butter has the ability to soften and smooth the skin by filling in the cracks of the skin’s top layer.
Ceramides- Helpful for restoring or protecting the skin’s barrier. This is essential to ensure that the hydration gained by the previous products is long term.
Similar to sensitive skin, it is important to avoid harsher chemicals which may strip the skin further. This includes alcohols, fragrances and exfoliants (both chemical and physical). Make sure to stay away from clay masks as well.
